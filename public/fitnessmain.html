Leg Exercises

1. Squats

Start standing with your feet hip-width apart. Lower yourself down by bending your knees. Go as far down as you can without letting your knees pass your toes. As you go down, keep your chest up but push your hips back. Then push back up and repeat. Do 3 sets, 10 reps each.

2. Lunges

Start standing with one foot in front and one foot behind. You should have most of the weight in the front foot and standing on your toes of the back foot. Lower yourself down by bending your knees. Go down all the way before your back knee touches the ground. Then push back up and repeat. Do 3 sets, 10 reps each on each leg.

3. Inner Thigh Lifts

Start by laying on your side. The leg on the ground should be stretched straight out and the other leg is bent overtop with your foot on the floor in front of you. You can lay your head and arms down or prop yourself up but keep your shoulders overtop of eachother. Then lift the leg that is on the ground up and then lower it down. Do 3 sets, 10 reps each.

4. Toe Risers

Start standing either with your feet apart or together. Use your toes to push up as high as you can go and then go back to the floor. Do 3 sets, 20 reps each.

5. Single Leg Squat

Start by standing on one foot while holding your other leg up in front of you. Lower yourself down by bending the grounded leg but do not let your lift foot touch the ground. Go as far down as possible without touching the ground. Then push yourself back up and repeat. If youre having trouble getting your balance then you can hold onto a stable object. Do 3 sets, 10 reps each on each leg.

Core Exercises

1. V-Ups

Start by lying on your back with your arms up by your ears. Then, simultaneously, lift your legs up while you sit up. Your body should make a 'V' shape and you should try to reach for your toes. Come back down and repeat. Do 3 sets, 10 reps each.

2. Crunches

Start by lying on your back with your arms down by your sides. Keep your shoulders and legs off the ground the whole time and make sure your back is flat on the ground (do not let your back arch). Then, simultaneously, sit up and bring your knees to your chest while trying to reach forwards. Come back down and repeat. Do 3 sets, 10 reps each.

3. Hollow Holds

Start by lying on your back with your arms down by your sides or up by your ears. Keep your shoulders and legs off the ground the whole time and make sure your back is flat on the ground (do not let your back arch). Do 3 sets, hold for 30 seconds.

4. Planks

Start by facing down with either your hands or forearms on the ground. Keep your shoulders overtop your hands or elbows. You should be on your toes and keep your body in a straight line (do not let your back arch). Do 3 sets, hold for 30 seconds.

5. Side Planks

Start with one side of your body towards the ground with either your hand or forearm holding you up. Keep your shoulders overtop your hand or elbow. You should be on the side of your body and keep your body in a straight line. Do 3 sets, hold for 30 seconds on each side.

6. Leg Lifts

Start by lying on your back with your arms by your side and palms on the floor. Put your legs up so you're in an 'L' shape. Slowly lower your legs down as far as they can go but do not let them touch the ground. Then bring your legs back up to the top. Your back should be completely flat on the ground the whole time(do not let your back arch). Do 3 sets, 10 reps each.

Arm Exercises

1. Push-Ups

Start by facing towards the ground with your hands and feet holding you up. You should be on your toes and your hands should be a bit more than shoulders width apart. Keep your back straight (do not let your back arch). Start to lower yourself down by bending your arms until they react a 90 degree angle. Your elbows should go straight out to the sides. Then push back up and repeat. Do 3 sets, 10 reps each.

2. Dips

You are going to need an elevated surface for this exercise (a table or chair will work). Start with your back facing your elevated surface you have chosen. Place your hands on the end of the surface with your fingers facing forwards. They would be about shoulder width apart. Place your feet infront of you with either straight or bent legs but make sure your feet are further more than your knees.(the lower the surface the less bent you want your legs to be.) Start to lower yourself down by bending your arms. Your elbows should go straight back. Then push back up and repeat. Do 3 sets, 10 reps each.

3. Arm Circles

Start by either standing or sitting with your arms straight out to the sides like the letter 'T'. Circle your arms clockwise with your arms straight. Do 3 sets, 1 minute each. Repeat and go counter-clockwise

4. Planks

Start by facing down with either your hands or forearms on the ground. Keep your shoulders overtop your hands or elbows. You should be on your toes and keep your body in a straight line (do not let your back arch). Do 3 sets, hold for 30 seconds.

Back Exercises

1. Supermans

Start by lying on your stomach with your arms up by your ears and legs out straight. Your feet should be together. Then, simultaneously, lift your arms and your legs off the ground while keeping your feet together. Then relax and lower back to the ground. Do 3 sets, 30 seconds each.

2. Aquaman

Start by lying on your stomach with your arms up by your ears and legs out straight. Then lift your right arm and your left leg off the ground. Then relax and lower back to the ground. Repeat with the left arm and right leg. Do 3 sets, 20 reps each (10 on each side).

3. Bridges

Start by lying on your back with your arms down by your side and legs bent up. Your feet should be hips width apart and the soles of your feet should be on the ground. Then push your hips up off the ground. Then relax and lower back to the ground. Do 3 sets, 15 reps each or 30 seconds each.
